# Business Model Tensions

## Current business model (as stated on site)

- Solo consultant, personal brand, Slovakia + international (EN/SK domains)
- Three offerings: Consulting, Implementation, Training
- Signature concept: agent-per-employee via Slack + CEO orchestrator + shared knowledge base
- Free 30-min discovery call is the only funnel entry
- Positioning: stack-agnostic, honest, fast ("days, not months")

## Reality

- Zero production deployments
- No case studies or social proof
- Building credibility via a 5-project pipeline (HN agent → doc processing → RAG KB → Clawforce polish → first pro bono client)
- Recent pivot from Clawforce company brand to personal name

## Tensions

### 1. Three services, one person, zero proof
Consulting, implementation, and training serve different buyers with different sales cycles. Hard to be credible at all three simultaneously as a solo with no track record.

### 2. Product vs. bespoke
The "agent per employee + CEO orchestrator + shared KB" is specific enough to productize, but sold as custom. Which is it?

### 3. Buyer is ambiguous
Slovak SMB? International mid-market? The two require very different pricing, sales motion, and delivery.

### 4. No pricing signal anywhere
Hurts qualification, attracts price-shoppers, costs you discovery calls.

### 5. Revenue shape
Project fees are implied. No retainer, no ongoing maintenance — which is where agent work actually pays long-term (agents drift, break, need tuning).

### 6. "Honest, not hyped" is table stakes, not a moat
The real distinctive thing — the opinionated agent-per-employee architecture — is buried.

### 7. "Days, not months" vs. FAQ's "2–6 weeks"
Small contradiction, but signals unsettled positioning.
